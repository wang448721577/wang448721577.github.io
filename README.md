  <li><b>Zhi-Wei Ma, Hong-jue Wang, Zhao-Long Hu, Xiang-Bin Zhu, Yi-Zhen Huang, Faliang Huang. DISLPSI: A framework for source localization in signed social networks with structural balance. Physics Letters A, 2024.</b></li>

  <li><b>Zhao-Long Hu, Hong-Jue Wang, Lei Sun, Chang-Bing Tang, Minglu Li. Source localization in complex networks with optimal observers based on maximum entropy sampling. Expert Systems With Applications, 2024.</b></li>

  <li><b>Shuyu Shao, Yang Zhang, Hongjue Wang, Xiaoli Fan. Virtual reality-induced motion sickness under autonomous vehicle driving conditions: EEG-based recognition and ANOVA analysis of various driving modes. Journal, 2024/2025.</b></li>

  <li><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-32832-w"><b>Wang, HJ., Hu, ZL., Tao, L. et al. The locatability of Pearson algorithm for multi-source location in complex networks. Scientific Reports, 13, 5692 (2023).</b></a>
  <br/><i>We study locating propagation sources in complex networks. We proposed a multi-source location algorithm for different propagation dynamics by using sparse observations. Without knowing the propagation dynamics and any dynamic parameters, we can calculate node centrality based on the positive correlation between inform time of nodes and geodesic distance between nodes and sources. The algorithm is robust and has high location accuracy for any number of sources. We study locatability of the proposed source location algorithm and present a corresponding strategy to select observer nodes based on greedy algorithm. All simulations on both model and real-world networks proved the feasibility and validity of this algorithm.</i></li>

  <li><b>Shui-Lin Peng, Hong-Jue Wang, Hao Peng, Xiang-Bin Zhu, Xiang Li, Jianmin Han, Dandan Zhao, Zhao-Long Hu. NLSI: An innovative method to locate epidemic sources on the SEIR propagation model. Chaos, 33, 083125 (2023).</b></li>

  <li><a href="https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1367-2630/ac8db3"><b>Wang Hongjue, Zhang Fangfeng. Locating sources of complex quantum networks. New Journal of Physics, 24, 103025 (2022).</b></a>
  <br/><i>The source location of quantum network is an important basic research in the direction of quantum networks, which has important scientific and application values in frontier fields including quantum state tomography, quantum computing, quantum communication, etc. In this paper, we conduct innovative research on quantum network source location algorithm and theory. A matrix vectorization technique is used to establish a linear system evolution model for quantum network system, and then a high-precision and high-efficiency source location algorithm based on compressed sensing is proposed for large-scale complex quantum networks. Numerical simulations on various model and real networks show the effectiveness and feasibility of the proposed algorithm.</i></li>

  <li><a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physleta.2021.127184"><b>Hong-Jue Wang, Fang-Feng Zhang, Kai-Jia Sun. An algorithm for locating propagation source in complex networks. Physics Letters A, 2021.</b></a>
  <br/><i>We study locating propagation source in complex networks. There are already many significant algorithms for locating source, but most of them need the propagation model in advance. In this paper, we propose three new algorithms to accurately locate the source by using only limited observed information without propagation model. The proposed algorithms are based on the defined estimated propagation delay in this paper. We study locatability of algorithms and propose an advisable strategy to select observer nodes based on greedy algorithm. All simulations on both model and empirical networks prove the feasibility and validity of the proposed algorithms.</i></li>

  <li><a href="https://epljournal.edpsciences.org/articles/epl/abs/2020/16/epl20259/epl20259.html"><b>Hong-Jue Wang, Kai-Jia Sun. Locating source of heterogeneous propagation model by universal algorithm. EPL, 131, 48001 (2020).</b></a>
  <br/><i>We focus on locating propagation source in complex networks. A novel and simple universal algorithm is proposed to accurately locate source of different heterogeneous propagation models by using only limited observed information without knowing anything about propagation models in advance. An observer nodes selecting strategy is proposed to improve the source locating accuracy of the proposed algorithm. Results simulated on model and empirical networks verify that the proposed source locating algorithm and selecting observer nodes strategy are effective and feasible.</i></li>

  <li><a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physa.2018.09.114"><b>Wang Hongjue. An universal algorithm for source location in complex networks. Physica A, 2019.</b></a>
  <br/><i>We study locating propagation source in complex networks. We proposed a universal algorithm, which can accurately locate source of different propagation dynamics by using sparse observations. Without knowing the propagation dynamics and any parameters, we calculate Spearman centrality based on the positive correlation between inform time of nodes and geodesic distance between nodes and source. The algorithm has high location accuracy with low time complexity and can accurately infer initial time of source. All simulations on both model and real-world networks proved the feasibility and validity of this algorithm.</i></li>

  <li><a href="https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0146727"><b>Tao Wang, Hongjue Wang, Xiaoxia Wang. CD-Based Indices for Link Prediction in Complex Network. PLOS ONE, 2016.</b></a>
  <br/><i>Lots of similarity-based algorithms have been designed to deal with the problem of link prediction. In order to improve prediction accuracy, a novel cosine similarity index CD based on distance between nodes and cosine value between vectors is proposed in this paper. A local community density index LD is also proposed. A series of CD-based indices are presented and applied in ten real networks. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of CD-based indices.</i></li>

  <li><a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0378437115005233"><b>Tao Wang, Hongjue Wang, Xiaoxia Wang. A novel cosine distance for detecting communities in complex networks. Physica A, 437: 21-35 (2015).</b></a>
  <br/><i>Detecting communities is significant to understand the potential structures and functions of complex systems. In order to detect communities more accurately and reasonably, a novel algorithm is proposed based on cosine distance and core-node. Experimental results on both real-world and synthetic networks demonstrate the feasibility and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.</i></li>

  <li><b>Tao Wang, Hongjue Wang, Yunzhu Li. Analysis of Cascading Failure Based on a New Load Redistribution Model. International Conference on Logistics Engineering, Management and Computer Science (LEMCS 2015), 2015.</b></li>
